# The Modernist Atlas

*The Modernist Atlas* is an open, long-term project dedicated to mapping the intellectual landscape of modernist studies (c. 1890–1941).

Rather than functioning as a conventional reading list, the Atlas traces the development of major ideas, concepts, methods, and debates that have shaped the field. Its aim is to provide a structured pathway towards a deep and interconnected understanding of literary modernism.

## The Atlas is organised around

- Reading pathways
- Major books
- Foundational essays
- Key concepts
- Major thinkers
- Critical methods
- Timelines
- Glossaries
- Connections between ideas

The project develops one book at a time. Each reading generates permanent notes, conceptual maps, and cross-references that contribute to an evolving knowledge base.

## Reading Framework

The following questions are used when reading every novel in the Modernist Atlas. They are designed to guide close reading, identify significant ideas, and generate permanent notes that contribute to the Atlas's interconnected knowledge base.

1. What is the novel's central formal or conceptual problem?

2. What are the novel's principal formal or structural principles?

3. Which recurring images, symbols, motifs, or patterns give the novel coherence?

4. How are individuals and their relationships represented?

5. How does the novel engage with its historical, cultural, political, or intellectual context?

6. What formal innovations distinguish the novel?

7. Which passages are essential for future research?

8. What new concepts or permanent ideas should be extracted into Atlas notes?

9. What connections does this novel have with other texts in the Atlas?
