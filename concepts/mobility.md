# Mobility

## Definition

Mobility refers to the movement of bodies through physical, social, and cultural space. In modernist studies, mobility is understood not simply as movement itself but as movement shaped by structures of power and as a practice through which space acquires meaning.

---

## Why it Matters

Mobility is a central concept in modernist studies because movement reveals how individuals experience class, gender, empire, identity, and urban modernity. Modernist writers frequently use movement not simply to relocate characters but to organise perception, construct narrative, and expose relationships of power.

---

## Key Questions

- Who is able to move?
- Who is prevented from moving?
- What forms of movement matter?
- Does movement reveal power?
- Does movement create meaning?
- Can movement itself become narrative?
