# Walking as Spatial Writing

**Source**

Collie, Natalie. "Walking in the City: Urban Space, Stories, and Gender."

---

## Permanent Idea

Walking is not simply movement through space but a form of spatial writing. Drawing on Michel de Certeau, Collie argues that everyday acts of walking transform abstract, planned places into lived spaces through memory, narrative, and embodied practice.

Urban space is therefore not a fixed geographical setting but something continually produced through movement. Walking becomes a creative act that writes meaning into the city.

---

## Why it Matters

This concept shifts attention from viewing space as a static backdrop to understanding it as an ongoing process. Movement does not merely occur within space; it actively produces space. This provides a powerful framework for analysing how modernist writers use bodily movement to construct urban experience.

## Research Reflection

This concept extends my own understanding of movement beyond locomotion. If walking functions as a form of spatial writing, Woolf's movement vocabulary of gesture, rhythm, dance, posture, and bodily orientation may likewise produce literary space. Rather than simply expressing meaning, movement itself becomes a narrative practice that generates relationships between bodies, places, and perception.
